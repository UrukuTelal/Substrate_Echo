# EXP-SOC-001: Cognitive Ecology Stability

**Date:** 2026-07-21
**Status:** Complete

## Hypothesis

Six agents with distinct personality genomes and selection pressures will maintain stable differentiation over time, rather than converging toward a universal "polite assistant" attractor.

## Method

6 agents (Cartographer, Engineer, Archivist, Philosopher, Trickster, Diplomat) with distinct genomes and selection pressures. 2000 interactions. Measured personality divergence, topic specialization, relationship formation, and innovation rate.

## Result

- **Personality divergence**: Stable (0.087 → 0.085). Homeostatic anchoring works.
- **Most distinct pair**: Archivist ↔ Trickster (0.172)
- **Most similar pair**: Cartographer ↔ Philosopher (0.024)
- **Trickster innovation**: 39.1% (27/69 concepts verified)
- **Diplomat**: 222 cooperations (highest — stabilizer role)
- **Relationships**: All agents form 5 relationships with trust differentiation

## Implication

Persona convergence is a real failure mode. Without homeostatic anchoring and genome-specific selection pressures, agents drift toward a universal "helpful assistant" attractor. The three-layer adaptation model (identity slow, behavior medium, reputation fast) with genome-specific rewards prevents this.
